EU AI Act readiness · risk versus penalty

You do not need to build AI to be exposed to AI regulation.

The EU AI Act uses a risk-based model. For SMEs, the practical question is not “do we have AI?” but: where does AI influence decisions, and could that usage become prohibited, high-risk, transparency-sensitive or minimal-risk?

Employment / HR decisions AI used for recruitment, selection, evaluation, promotion, scheduling or performance signals.
Credit, pricing or eligibility AI used to score customers, decide eligibility, pricing, fraud or risk treatment.
Customer-facing AI / chatbots Users interact with AI or receive AI-generated advice, messages or recommendations.
Vendor AI inside services Suppliers use AI inside managed services, HR tools, CRM, support, SOC, marketing or analytics.
Biometric / surveillance use AI used for identification, monitoring, access, behaviour analysis or safety/security surveillance.
